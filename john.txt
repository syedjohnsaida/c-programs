1. Decide the Type of Company
Choose the type of company to register:

Private Limited Company (most common)
Public Limited Company
One Person Company (OPC)
Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)
Sole Proprietorship
Section 8 Company (Non-profit)
2. Obtain a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
Since all forms are submitted online, every proposed director or designated partner must have a DSC:

Obtain a DSC from a government-recognized certifying authority (e.g., eMudhra, Sify).
Documents needed: PAN card, Aadhaar card, photo, email ID, and phone number.
3. Apply for a Director Identification Number (DIN)
A DIN is required for all directors.
Apply via the SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically) form on the MCA portal.
Documents needed: Identity proof, address proof, and a passport-sized photo.
4. Reserve a Name (Optional but Recommended)
Use the RUN (Reserve Unique Name) service on the MCA portal to propose a company name.
Follow the Companies Act, 2013 guidelines:
The name should be unique.
Avoid prohibited or restricted terms.
Approval typically takes 1-2 days.
5. Draft the Memorandum and Articles of Association (MOA & AOA)
MOA: Outlines the company’s objectives and scope of work.
AOA: Defines the company’s internal rules and regulations.
6. File for Incorporation
Use the SPICe+ Form on the MCA portal. This integrated form includes:

Company registration.
DIN allotment.
PAN and TAN application.
GSTIN (optional).
Required Documents:
MOA & AOA
ID and address proof of directors
Address proof of the registered office (e.g., utility bill, rental agreement)
No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the owner if the premises are rented.
7. Certificate of Incorporation
Once the MCA approves the application, you'll receive a Certificate of Incorporation with the Corporate Identification Number (CIN).
This serves as legal proof of your company's existence.
8. Post-Incorporation Compliance
Apply for:
PAN and TAN (automatic via SPICe+ form).
GST Registration (if applicable).
Open a bank account in the company’s name.
Maintain statutory books and registers.
Appoint an auditor within 30 days.
Timeline and Costs
Time: Approximately 7-15 days, depending on document preparation and processing.
Cost:
Professional fees (if using a consultant): ₹5,000 - ₹15,000+
MCA fees: Varies with company type and capital
